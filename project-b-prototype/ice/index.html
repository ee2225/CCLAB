Ice

Ice is the solid state of water. It forms when water freezes at or below 0 degrees Celsius. It is essential to life on Earth and plays a crucial role in shaping our planet's climate, geography, and ecosystems.

Examples of everyday uses of ice:

  • Preserving food
  • Cooling beverages
  • First aid

If you want to learn more about ice and its magic, I highly recommend watching the 2013 animated film, named "Frozen".

Ice is also the foundation for a variety of winter sports and activities, including ice hockey, figure skating, and curling, bringing people together to enjoy the thrill of cold-weather fun.

"The teddy bear tradition" in figure skating is a heartwarming practice where spectators throw teddy bears onto the ice after a skater completes a successful performance. It acts as a display of appreciation and support for the skater's performance. After the teddy bears are thrown onto the ice, they are typically collected and donated to charity organizations or children's hospitals, bringing joy to those in need!
