What Was Water?

Water is a transparent, tasteless, odorless, and nearly colorless chemical substance that is the main constituent of Earth's streams, lakes, oceans, and rivers. It is vital for all known forms of life, serving as a solvent for many substances, as well as being an essential part of various biochemical processes. Chemically, water is composed of two hydrogen atoms bonded to one oxygen atom, forming the chemical formula H2O.

It exists in three states:

  • Solid (ice)
  • Liquid (water)
  • Gas (vapor)
